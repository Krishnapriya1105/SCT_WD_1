Food delivery is the service of bringing food to a customer's location, either at home or another place.
It can be provided by restaurants, stores, or independent food delivery companies.

Customers can order food by phone, through a mobile app or website, or by using a third-party food ordering service.
An order is typically made either by telephone, through the supplier's website or mobile app, or through a third party food ordering service.

The delivered items can include entrees, sides, drinks, desserts, or grocery items and are typically delivered in boxes or bags.
The delivery person will normally drive a car, but in bigger cities where homes and restaurants are closer together, they may use bikes or motorized scooters.

Due to shifting habits in response to lockdowns and restrictions from the COVID-19 pandemic, online food delivery through third-party companies has become a growing industry and caused a "delivery revolution."
